Lysa 4.0 debuts new album with hit single "Dreamwave 42"

Date: Nov. 12, 2099

By: M. Harper, Music Correspondent

Lysa 4.0, the innovative electronic music duo, is back with a bang, debuting their highly anticipated new album 50 Reasons to Love alongside the release of their hit single "Dreamwave 42." Known for their genre-blending sound and hyper-modern production techniques, Lysa 4.0 continues to push the boundaries of modern music with their latest work, marking a bold new chapter in their career.

Their Origins

Lysa 4.0's origins are as intriguing as their music. The duo, composed of producers Lysa Adams and Finnley Kade, met in an unlikely but serendipitous way.

Lysa Adams, born American and raised in Paris, is a classically trained pianist turned electronic producer. She had released a few songs and had collaborated with other artists in Paris such as Luna Novus and Pherom*ne, and was working on a solo project when she stumbled upon Kade’s work while browsing an online forum dedicated to experimental music.

Finnley Kade, originally from New York, moved to Neo-Metro at 16 to start his music career. In Neo-Metro, he similarly worked with other artists such as Transcendia (with whom he released the iconic Neo-Neo-Metro), Darius Dalton, and Miko Chrome. A self-taught beatmaker with a background in graphic design and sound design, he gained attention for his immersive, glitchy beats and his unique approach to blending retro and futuristic sounds.

At first, Lysa and Finnley's connection was purely digital—sharing ideas, remixing each other’s tracks, and trading production tips. Their initial collaborations were small, experimental projects. But it quickly became clear that their creative synergy was something special. They both shared a love for the same kind of atmospheric, genre-defying music that blends elements of ambient, synthwave, and house, and they were both obsessed with pushing the limits of what electronic music could be.

After a year of chatting online, they finally met up in Berlin to actually work together, and Lysa 4.0 was born shortly after, combining their individual strengths—Lysa’s classical training and ear for melody with Finnley’s mastery of electronic textures and beats. Their first EP, released under the name Lysa 4.0, was an instant hit among underground electronic music communities, gaining attention for its unique fusion of old-school synths and cutting-edge production techniques.

When asked why they named themselves Lysa 4.0, Finnley said he was "really shy, and didn't want to put [his] name out there. [He] had no interest in being famous". Lysa, on the other hand, had no issue, saying "I want my parents to see my name on billboards. It would be a giant "fuck-you" to them because they never believed in me." The "4.0" comes from the fact that Lysa had released 3 solo projects before, so this next venture would be her fourth.

Their New Single

"Dreamwave 42" stands out as a perfect introduction to their latest album 50 Reasons to Love, encapsulating the duo’s signature mix of ambient synths, pulsating basslines, and ethereal vocals. It also features the rare inclusion of Finnley's raspy singing voice. Him and Lysa sing duet, playing opposing sides of a relationship which ended due to unfaithfulness and resentment. Many fans theorize Kade wrote the song about his breakup earlier this year with actress Alissa Moran, who began dating Oliwier Robbins only 2 months after ending a 3-year long relationship with Kade.

However, it's not all doom-and-gloom. Lysa recently hinted that Finnley may be in a new relationship, posting a picture on her social media of Finnley and her friend Synthia X as the three of them hung out in Paris. Fans of Synthia X have been theorizing that she's in a new relationship, particularly after the release of her new single "Hypernova"in which she sings about "finding love in Paris".

In any case, Lysa 4.0's new album, which is expected to explore themes of nostalgia, digital identity, and heartbreak, is already generating buzz for its innovation and sonic depth. Lysa 4.0 has teased that "Dreamwave 42" serves as a sonic portal to the album's broader narrative—a journey through a futuristic landscape where human experience and technology intertwine.


50 Reasons to Love is available for streaming now.
